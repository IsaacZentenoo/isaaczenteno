Facts of Madrid

Madrid is the vibrant capital of Spain, known for its rich history, lively culture, world-class museums, and bustling nightlife. It blends elegant boulevards and expansive parks with modern life, making it one of Europe’s most dynamic cities. The city is also world-famous for its passion for football, being home to two legendary clubs: Real Madrid and Atlético de Madrid, which bring fans together from all over the globe.

Country: Spain

Population: 3.3 million in the city; 6.7 million in the metro area

Facts of Mazatlan

Mazatlán is a lively coastal city in Sinaloa, Mexico, celebrated for its golden beaches, warm hospitality, and unique blend of tradition and modernity. Known as the "Pearl of the Pacific," it combines historic charm in its beautifully restored Old Town with the vibrant energy of its bustling Malecón, one of the longest oceanfront promenades in the world. The city is also famous for its thriving seafood cuisine, colorful festivals like Carnival, and a rich cultural scene. Passion for sports runs deep here too, especially baseball, with the local team Venados de Mazatlán inspiring loyal fans across the region.

State / Province: Sinaloa. Mazatlán is a city and municipality located in the southern part of the state of Sinaloa, Mexico

Country: Mexico

Population: Municipality: 501,441 (2020 census)

Facts of Hamburg

Hamburg is a dynamic port city in northern Germany, renowned for its maritime heritage, cultural diversity, and modern architecture. As the country’s second-largest city, it blends historic landmarks like the Speicherstadt warehouse district—a UNESCO World Heritage Site—with contemporary hubs such as the Elbphilharmonie concert hall. Its lively nightlife thrives in the famous Reeperbahn, while expansive parks and canals give it a unique balance of energy and tranquility. Hamburg is also home to passionate football culture, with clubs like Hamburger SV and FC St. Pauli fueling the city’s deep sporting spirit.

State / Province: Hamburg (city-state)

Country: Germany

Population: 1.96 million (end of 2023); ~1,862,565 (end of 2024)

Latitude & Longitude:53.5511° N, 9.9937° E

Facts of Munich

Munich is the vibrant capital of Bavaria, Germany, celebrated for its rich history, stunning architecture, and lively traditions. Nestled near the Alps, the city combines centuries-old landmarks like the Marienplatz and Nymphenburg Palace with modern innovation and a thriving cultural scene. Munich is world-famous for Oktoberfest, attracting millions with its festive spirit, music, and beer culture, while also being a hub for art, technology, and education. Sports passion runs deep here too, with FC Bayern Munich standing as one of the most successful football clubs in the world, uniting fans across the globe.

State / Province: Bavaria (capital of the state)

Country: Germany

Population: 1,604,384 (as of Nov 30, 2024); or ~1,505,005 (as of Dec 31, 2024)

Latitude & Longitude:Approx. 48.137° N, 11.576° E

Facts of Paris

Paris is the enchanting capital of France, renowned for its timeless elegance, iconic landmarks, and world-class art and culture. The city seamlessly blends historic beauty—from the Eiffel Tower and Notre-Dame Cathedral to the charming streets of Montmartre—with modern life, gourmet cuisine, and a vibrant café culture. Paris is also a global hub of fashion, literature, and music, while football fans celebrate clubs like Paris Saint-Germain, adding to the city’s dynamic and passionate spirit.
